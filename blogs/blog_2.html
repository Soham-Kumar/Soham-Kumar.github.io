Man is condemned to be free

"Man is condemned to be free. Condemned, because he did not create himself; in other respect is free because, once thrown into the world, he is responsible for everything he does."
~ Jean-Paul Sartre, Existentialism and Human Emotions
"Condemned to be free" may sound paradoxical, but it encapsulates the human condition: compelled to act yet burdened with justifying those actions. Humanity stands between animals and gods—neither bound by pure instinct nor entirely free of it. This freedom demands reflection and this is what defines the human life - the need to find a raison d'être to navigate the sufferings of daily existence.3

The biology, the nature doesn't owe you a beautiful existence, it doesn't owe you a life that you will like - its optimized to perpetuate itself, and not for the satisfaction of the individuals that constitute it. But it does realize that a one needs some trinkets to be pleasured with to keep the machinery running. You are lucky if these trinkets take you far in life, but there must come a time sooner or later that these trinkets start looking as what they truly are - childish playthings. Upon hitting this realization, good and bad both times start having an undercurrent of deep, unwavering and surreal feeling of meaninglessness. The second consequence (or the second order effect) of this realization is the loss of home. Frued talks about the loss of a loved object through disappointment and how it leads to losing a part of yourself and its relation to melancholy.4 This loss of meaning is imminent to come to everyone, but to some death comes first. Who is luckier: one who lives merrily, deceived by fleeting illusions, or the one burdened by the weight of their falsity?

But why doesn't life have an inherent meaning? Because asking for the meaning of life is asking for a linear answer to a circular question.

Person A: Why do you exist?

Person B: ...well, I exist because my parents reproduced.

Person A: That doesn't explain why your parents exist. I could just keep asking why to all of your responses all the way back to the Big Bang, at which point you'll essentially be saying because existence began to which I will ask why did existence begin, and we will have arrived back at square one. So, I'll ask again why, why are you?"

Person B: Uhmmm I exist to bring my future children into this world.

Person A: We have the same issue again just in the opposite direction. I can keep asking why until we get to the inevitable heat death of the universe and you'll run out of answers. That's exactly the issue though. That's why I'm saying - there's no meaning to any of it. It's all for nothing. Looking backwards it's accidents all the way back and there's no meaning to any of it looking forwards, it all comes to an end. So that settles it then - that's your answer - that it's all for nothing. Both the ends of the thread of time are loose and all you have to do is pull, and the whole damn thing unravels back to the present moment. There's no real point in getting out of bed in the morning, no point in having kids, my parents gave birth to me for no reason at all, and it's all for nothing.

What's it ALL for? It can't all be for something else because then you wouldn't have been talking about IT ALL, would you? Your question cannot be answered without referring to something within your expression IT ALL. For even if I did give you an answer that referred to something outside of IT ALL, like heaven for instance, as soon as its existence was an established fact it would be contained within the expression IT ALL.

Listen, Little Man!

“You'll have a good, secure life when being alive means more to you than security, love more than money, your freedom more than public or partisan opinion, when the mood of Beethoven's or Bach's music becomes the mood of your whole life … when your thinking is in harmony, and no longer in conflict, with your feelings … when you let yourself be guided by the thoughts of great sages and no longer by the crimes of great warriors … when you pay the men and women who teach your children better than the politicians; when truths inspire you and empty formulas repel you; when you communicate with your fellow workers in foreign countries directly, and no longer through diplomats…”
